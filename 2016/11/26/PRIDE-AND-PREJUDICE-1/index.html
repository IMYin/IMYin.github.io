'Chapter 1'

It is a truth universally acknowledged, that a single man in possession(领地，所有，拥有，n.) of a good fortune, must be in want of a wife.

我们一直坚信这样一个真理：当一个单身汉阔绰了，总是在想着娶一位妻子。

However little known the feelings or views of such a man may be on his first entering a neighbourhood, this truth is so well fixed(固定) in the minds of the surrounding families, that he is considered the rightful(合法的) property of some one or other of their daughters.

当有这样的单身汉出现时，虽然周围的邻居对他一点也不了解，但这样根深蒂固的观念早已将这个单身汉视为他们女儿的合法财产。

“My dear Mr. Bennet,” said his lady to him one day, “have you heard that Netherfield Park is let(出租) at last?”

有一天，妻子对丈夫说道：“亲爱的Bennet，你有没有听说　Netherfield Park 终于被租出去了？”

Mr. Bennet replied that he had not.

他丈夫说没有。

“But it is,” returned she; “for Mrs. Long has just been here, and she told me all about it.”

“它确实租出去了，”他太太说道，“Long小姐刚刚得知这件事后，全都告诉我了。”

Mr. Bennet made no answer.

Bennet没吱声。

“Do you not want to know who has taken it?” cried his wife impatiently(不耐烦).

“你不想知道是谁租了它吗？”，他妻子不耐烦的嘟囔道。

“You want to tell me, and I have no objection(异议，拒绝之理由) to hearing it.”

“你要是想说，我也找不到拒听的理由。”

This was invitation enough.

这句话足具挑逗性。

“Why, my dear, you must know, Mrs. Long says that Netherfield is taken by a young man of large fortune from the north of England; that he came down on Monday in a chaise(躺椅，二轮轻便马车，四轮游览马车) and four to see the place, and was so much delighted(愉悦的) with it, that he agreed with Mr. Morris immediately; that he is to take possession before Michaelmas, and some of his servants(仆人) are to be in the house by the end of next week.”

“哦，亲爱的，Long太太说Netherfield被英格兰北部的一个阔少租下了。周一那天他坐着一辆四轮游览马车来看房子，过程中非常愉悦，并且很爽快地答应了Morris先生要租下这里。他打算赶在米迦勒节之前搬进来，在下周末之前先让他的仆人们搬过来。”

“What is his name?”

“他叫什么？”

“Bingley.”

“Is he married or single?”

“结婚了吗？”

“Oh! Single, my dear, to be sure! A single man of large fortune; four or five thousand a year. What a fine thing for our girls!”

“噢！我敢肯定是单身！简直是钻石王老五，一年有四、五千的收入。对我们女儿们来讲是多么好的一件事啊!”

“How so? How can it affect them?”

“这话怎么讲，跟女儿们有什么关系？”

“My dear Mr. Bennet,” replied his wife, “how can you be so tiresome(烦人的)! You must know that I am thinking of his marrying one of them.”

“亲爱的Bennet先生，”他妻子说道，“你怎么这么木！我可是在想着他能娶我们的其中一个女儿做妻子。”

“Is that his design in settling(沉降) here?”

“他搬到这里是这样打算的？”

“Design! Nonsense, how can you talk so! But it is very likely that he may fall in love with one of them, and therefore you must visit him as soon as he comes.”

“打算！胡扯，你怎么能这么说！但是他很可能爱上我们其中一个女儿，所以他以来你就得去拜访他。”

“I see no occasion(场合、时机) for that. You and the girls may go, or you may send them by themselves, which perhaps will be still better, for as you are as handsome as any of them, Mr. Bingley may like you the best of the party.”

“我看没那机会。应该你和孩子们去，或者你让孩子们自己去，那样也许会更好一些，你长得和孩子们一样漂亮，Bingley兴许会对你有好感。”

“My dear, you flatter(吹捧、奉承) me. I certainly have had my share of beauty, but I do not pretend(假装、伪装) to be anything extraordinary(非凡的、超凡的) now. When a woman has five grown-up(成年的) daughters, she ought to give over thinking of her own beauty.”

“亲爱的，你在捧我呢吧。我年轻时确实漂亮过，不过现在在众人面前没法再显摆了。当一个女人有了５个成年的女儿，也就对自己的容颜不抱有什么幻想了。”

“In such cases, a woman has not often much beauty to think of.”

“这么说，女人的美貌还是经不起时间的考验啊。”

“But, my dear, you must indeed go and see Mr. Bingley when he comes into the neighbourhood.”

“不过话说回来，亲爱的，当Bingley先生搬过来时你很有必要去见见他。”

“It is more than I engage(从事，聘用) for, I assure you.”

“老实说，这并不是我分内的事。”

“But consider your daughters. Only think what an establishment(建立，树立；n.) / it would be for one of them. Sir William and Lady Lucas are determined to go, merely on that account, for in general, you know, they visit no newcomers. Indeed you must go, for it will be impossible for us to visit him if you do not.”

“但是你为女儿们想想，要是她们其中一个能攀上这个单身汉，该是多么好的一件事啊。William一家决定要去，就是为此缘故，要知道平时他们可从来不拜访新邻居。你得去，不然我们母女们也没法儿去拜访他。”

“You are over-scrupulous(过分谨慎), surely. I dare say Mr. Bingley will be very glad to see you; and I will send a few lines by you to assure him of my hearty(爽朗的，诚恳的) consent(同意，准许) to his marrying whichever he chooses of the girls; though I must throw in a good word for my little Lizzy.”

“你太多虑了。我敢说Bingley见到你肯定很高兴，到时候让你稍封信给他，就说无论他选我们哪个女儿做妻子我都非常愿意。不过我得为小Lizzy多美言两句。”

“I desire you will do no such thing. Lizzy is not a bit better than the others; and I am sure she is not half so handsome as Jane, nor half so good-humoured as Lydia. But you are always giving her the preference(偏爱).”

“你可别这么做，Lizzy不如其他孩子优秀，并且我确信她张得连Jane的一半都比不上，幽默感也没Lydia的一般强。但是你总是偏爱她。”

“They have none of them much to recommend them,” replied he; “they are all silly and ignorant like other girls; but Lizzy has something more of quickness than her sisters.”

“她们身上没什么可圈可点的，”丈夫说道，“她们跟其他女孩一样笨并且无知，Lizzy跟她的姐妹们比起来倒是更利索些。”

“Mr. Bennet, how can you abuse(谩骂) your own children in such a way? You take delight in vexing me(你就喜欢气我). You have no compassion(同情心) for my poor nerves(神经).”

“Bennet先生，你怎么可以这么说你的孩子？你就喜欢气我，对我脆弱的神经一点同情心都没有。”

“You mistake me, my dear. I have a high respect for your nerves. They are my old friends. I have heard you mention them with consideration(照顾，关心，体谅) these last twenty years at least.”

“你错怪我了，亲耐的。我可是非常尊重你的神经。它们是我的老朋友。至少在过去二十年我总是耐着性子听你提起它们。”

“Ah, you do not know what I suffer.”

“哎，你并不知道我有多受罪。”

“But I hope you will get over it, and live to see many young men of four thousand a year come into the neighbourhood.”

“但是我希望你会好起来的，并且亲眼看到有很多每年4000收入的阔少来这里成为你的邻居。”

“It will be no use to us, if twenty such should come, since you will not visit them.”

“如果你不去拜访他们，即使搬来二十个，对我们也没什么用。”

“Depend upon it(放心), my dear, that when there are twenty, I will visit them all.”

“放心吧，亲爱的，如果搬来二十个，我肯定会去拜访他们的。”

Mr. Bennet was so odd a mixture of quick parts, sarcastic(讽刺的) humour, reserve, and caprice(任性的), that the experience of three-and-twenty years had been insufficient(不足的，支绌) to make his wife understand his character(特点，性格). Her mind was less difficult to develop. She was a woman of mean(卑劣、吝啬) understanding, little information, and uncertain temper(脾气). When she was discontented, she fancied herself nervous. The business of her life was to get her daughters married; its solace(抚慰，慰藉) was visiting and news.

Bennet是一个古怪的复杂混合体，诙谐幽默、缄默、任性，他妻子花了二十三年都没有琢磨透他的性格。他太太的性格就没那么难琢磨了。吝啬的理解能力、孤陋寡闻、喜怒无常。当她不高兴时，总是神经兮兮的。她活着就是为了忙着把她的女儿们嫁出去，走访和探信儿成了她聊以慰藉的事情。
